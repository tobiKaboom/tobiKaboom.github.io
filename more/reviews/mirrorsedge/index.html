Mirror's Edge - Game Review

General Info

Developer: Dice Year of release: 2008 Genre: Platformer Platform played on: PS3 Should you play it: Yes! Mirror's edge is a 3d platformer higly focused on movement and parkour in higly crammed and urban landscapes. The playtime is about 3-4 hours, but with a high replayability. It is structured in 11 levels: a tutorial, which somehow still has a lot of things to do, a prologue, and 9 chapters.

thoughts

Although it's a 2009 game, the graphics are astonishing: while the character models are nothing to write home about and the textures are not in the highest of resolutions, the overall environment and elements look beautiful and different from the games of the era: instead of the dirty and grey worlds of GTA V or Watchdogs, Mirror's Edge is built of white beautiful concrete and vibrant colors. The gameplay is very fun and fast paced. There are some combat sections, which are still very fun but do break the rythm of the game: usually you can either choose to disarm your opponents (Even using slow-mo to do it easier), or fighting them. The weapons have a semi-realistic weight / ease to handle mechanic, which prevents parkouring which heavier guns and rifles. Something i didn't like at all about the game were the cutscenes: most these didn't use the game's defined art stile, but a 2d animated corporate-ish style that looks really bad, especially compared to the great looking in-engine cutscenes. The game follows a fairly linear structure closer to that of puzzle games than to that of shooters, as there is not a final bossfight but the level design just becomes harder and harder, along with the (At times, fairly annoying) police encounters.
It's a game you should absolutely play if you like 3d platformers at all, as it still looks amazing and is tons of fun.
